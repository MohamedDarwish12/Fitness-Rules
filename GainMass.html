Gaining Muscle Mass- where to start

Increase the Calories

In contrast to achieing a lean physique, you would wabt a positive calorie balance instead (consuming more than you burn.) However, additional calories outside of the body's average limit leads to excess fat gains. To avoid this, a daily increase of 250 to 500 calories is reccomended.

Training immensely elevates protein turnover. (see section 'your routine guide' for more tips!).

Use this calorie calculator to estimate the number of calories you would need to consume each day.


diet

Incorporate protein to build and maintain muscle mass. For eveery 454g of body-weight, you should be looking to eat 1g of protein. Say that the average person weighs 71kg, they are expected to consume 156.39g of protein.


In this case, it is more beneficial to include a whole protein source with each meal, such as:

  • Red Meat: Beef, Pork, Lamb.
  • Fish: Tuna, Salmon, Mackerel.
  • Poultry: Chicken, Poultry, Duck.


Eat Healthy fats

Healthy fats improve fat loss and overall health as they digest slowly. Make sure to avoid artificial transfats ad margarine


Whole Foods should be the predominant meal

In order to boost muscle mass significantly, 90% of food inake should include wholefoods (unprocessed and unrefined food that come in their natural state). Avoid processed food as much as possible.


Don't Skip Out on Breakfast

Eating well early in the morning has been proven to aid weight management, with the consumption of protein speeding up your body's recovery and promoting lean muscle growth.
