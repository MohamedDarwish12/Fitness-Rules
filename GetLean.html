The lean physique- where to start

Cut the Calories

Weightloss is largely determined by calorie intake and control. This is achieved through consuming less calories than the amount you burn each day.


A calorie deficit is often the way to go. The initial step is to determine how many calories you should be cosuming each day to lose weight.


Eating around 500 fewer calories than you need every day can help you drop the kilos.


On average, young women ages betweeen 19 to 30 are reccomended that they eat between 2,200-2,400 calories. 31 and above should consume up to 2,200 calories while sedentary women base diets aorund 1,800 calories.


Young men 19 to 30 may eat 2,600 to 2,800 calories. 31 and above are suggested to consime 3,000 and sedentary men intake 2,000 calories.


Use this calorie calculator to estimate the number of calories you would need to consume each day.


Consume sufficient protein

Protein stimulates appetite inducing hormones GLP-1, peptide YY and cholecystokinin, resulting in an automatic reduction in calorie intake. An additional benefit is that it helps you feel full while on a calorie deficit.


Lean protein foods include chicken, fish, beans, lentils, etc.


Focus on your Body Fat Percentage

Weight loss refers to a decrease in the overall body weight and is dependent on a variety of factors including dieting and excercise. Fat loss, on the other hand refers to weight lost as a result of reduction in fat and is best accomplished through a combination of regular excercise and a healthy diet that is low in diet and high in protein.
